Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 16, 2026

CalPaper ("the App") is developed and operated by Balazs Huszar ("we", "us", "our"). This Privacy Policy explains how CalPaper processes data when you use the App.

This Privacy Policy also explains how the public CalPaper website at https://calpaper.eu/ handles optional website analytics and cookie consent choices.

1. Overview

CalPaper is a Windows desktop application that connects to user-selected calendar sources and renders calendar information on your desktop wallpaper.

CalPaper does not operate its own backend for collecting or storing your calendar, weather, or location data. Most processing happens locally on your device. When you use connected features, the App communicates directly from your device with third-party services such as calendar providers, weather providers, geolocation providers, ICS hosts, and the Microsoft Store.

2. Data We Process

Depending on which features you use, CalPaper may process the following categories of data:

  • Calendar event titles, start times, end times, all-day status, and reminder metadata
  • Calendar names, colors, calendar identifiers, and connected account identifiers
  • Connection details for imported calendar sources, such as ICS feed URLs
  • Authentication data for connected services, such as OAuth access and refresh tokens for Google and Microsoft
  • Apple Calendar account email address and app-specific password for CalDAV connections
  • For Google connections, Google account email address, Google Calendar list metadata, and read-only calendar event/reminder data requested through Google APIs
  • Weather-related location data, including coordinates, region/country hints, timezone hints, and weather results
  • User preferences such as language, theme, layout, visible calendars, wallpaper output settings, and notification settings
  • Diagnostic runtime log entries generated by the desktop app

Supported calendar sources currently include:

  • Google Calendar
  • Microsoft Outlook Calendar
  • Apple Calendar via CalDAV
  • ICS/iCalendar calendar sources

3. How We Use the Data

CalPaper uses processed data only to provide the App's functionality, including:

  • Connecting to calendars selected by you
  • Fetching and caching calendar events for display on your wallpaper
  • Rendering calendar layouts, themes, colors, and per-monitor wallpapers
  • Showing event reminders and notification text when the alarms feature is enabled
  • Fetching and displaying weather information when the weather feature is enabled
  • Saving your settings so the App can remain configured across launches
  • Checking premium purchase state through Microsoft Store APIs
  • Writing limited diagnostic logs needed to troubleshoot desktop runtime issues

Google user data is used only to provide user-facing calendar synchronization, calendar selection, and wallpaper display features inside CalPaper.

We do not use your app data for advertising, behavioral profiling, selling user data, or training generalized artificial intelligence or machine learning models.

4. Calendar and Authentication Services

To connect external calendars, the App communicates directly with the relevant provider from your device:

  • Google OAuth and Google Calendar APIs
  • Microsoft OAuth and Microsoft Graph Calendar APIs
  • Apple iCloud CalDAV endpoints
  • Third-party or self-hosted ICS/iCalendar feed URLs that you add

For Google and Microsoft, authentication uses OAuth flows handled between your device and the provider. For Apple Calendar, the App uses the email address and app-specific password that you provide for CalDAV access. We do not receive your primary Google, Microsoft, or Apple account password through our own servers.

For Google Calendar connections, CalPaper requests only the data needed to let you sign in, identify the connected account, list calendars, and read calendar content that you choose to display in the App.

5. Weather and Location Data

If you use the weather feature, CalPaper attempts to determine an approximate location so it can request current weather data.

Location resolution currently uses the following order:

  • Windows system location APIs, if available and permitted
  • IP-based geolocation services
  • System region/timezone hints combined with geocoding

Weather and location-related requests may involve third-party services such as:

  • Open-Meteo weather API
  • Open-Meteo geocoding API
  • ipapi.co
  • ipwho.is

Weather snapshots are cached locally and may include the fetch time, temperature, weather code, approximate coordinates, location source, and location hint text.

6. Local Storage

CalPaper stores data locally on your device for functionality and performance. Local files may include:

  • %AppData%\Calpaper\settings.json for settings, connected sources, and related configuration. Sensitive fields in this file, including OAuth tokens, Apple CalDAV credentials, ICS URLs, account identifiers, and connected account emails, are protected at rest using Windows DPAPI and are tied to the current Windows user account.
  • %AppData%\Calpaper\events-cache.json for cached calendar events and alarm metadata. This cache is stored as a Windows DPAPI-protected local payload.
  • %AppData%\Calpaper\weather-cache.json for cached weather and location-related data
  • %AppData%\Calpaper\theme-assets\ for prepared local image assets used during wallpaper rendering
  • %AppData%\Calpaper\app.log for runtime diagnostic logging
  • %AppData%\Calpaper\wallpaper.png and rendered wallpaper outputs used by the App

Some diagnostic logging is reduced in packaged Microsoft Store builds, but local storage still remains part of the App's normal operation.

7. Data Sharing

We do not operate our own server that receives your calendar or weather data.

However, the App does send data directly from your device to third-party services when you use related features, including:

  • Calendar providers you connect
  • Weather and geolocation providers used by the weather feature
  • ICS feed hosts that you configure
  • Microsoft Store services for purchase validation and billing

We do not sell your data, rent your data, or run advertising networks inside the App. We do not transfer Google user data to third parties except as needed to provide the functionality you request or as required by law.

8. Third-Party Services

CalPaper may rely on third-party services that have their own privacy terms, including:

  • Google
  • Microsoft
  • Apple
  • Open-Meteo
  • ipapi.co
  • ipwho.is
  • Google Analytics, but only on the public website and only after you opt in
  • Providers or hosts of ICS/iCalendar feeds that you choose to import

CalPaper does not use third-party analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or crash reporting SDKs in the current desktop app build.

The sentence above applies to the desktop app build. Separately, the public website may use Google Analytics for limited traffic measurement only after you explicitly accept analytics through the cookie banner.

9. Website Cookies and Analytics

The public CalPaper website uses a consent banner for optional analytics cookies and similar browser storage. If you choose to allow them, Google Analytics may collect limited website usage data such as page views, approximate location derived from IP, device and browser details, and referring page information.

We use this information only to understand overall site traffic, improve content, and make the website work better for visitors.

If you decline optional cookies, the website remains available and analytics are not loaded. Your choice is stored locally in your browser so we can remember your preference across visits.

If you previously allowed optional cookies, you can withdraw consent at any time by using the Cookie Settings link in the website footer.

10. Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational steps to reduce unnecessary exposure of your data by:

  • Keeping most calendar, weather, and rendering processing on your local device
  • Connecting directly to providers rather than routing Google Calendar or other provider data through our own backend
  • Using provider-supported authentication flows such as OAuth with PKCE where applicable
  • Using HTTPS/TLS for data transmitted between the App and supported provider APIs
  • Protecting sensitive locally stored credentials and calendar cache data at rest with Windows DPAPI, scoped to the current Windows user account
  • Limiting normal logging in packaged Store builds

Windows DPAPI protection means sensitive local values are designed to be readable only by the same Windows user account on the same device. If local app files are copied to another Windows account or another device, protected credentials and protected event cache contents may no longer be readable.

While we apply these protections to sensitive local credentials and cached calendar data, other local files such as wallpaper output, theme assets, weather cache data, and logs may still exist in unencrypted local form depending on the feature used.

11. Data Retention

Data is retained locally for as long as it remains in CalPaper's local files or is needed for the App to function.

You can reduce or remove locally stored data by:

  • Disconnecting or removing calendar sources in the App
  • Turning off features you do not use, such as weather or alarms
  • Uninstalling the App
  • Deleting the local AppData folder at %AppData%\Calpaper, if you want to remove cached files, settings, and logs manually

When you remove a connected calendar source or account, the associated locally stored authentication credentials for that source are removed from the App's active configuration. Existing older local files, backups, or manually copied files remain under your control.

12. Legal Basis for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we rely on the following legal bases where applicable:

  • Performance of a contract for processing needed to provide the App's requested functionality
  • Legitimate interests for local caching, rendering, diagnostics, and security-related operation of the App
  • Consent where you choose to enable optional features such as calendar connections, weather/location-based functionality, or optional website analytics cookies

13. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights relating to access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, or portability of personal data.

Because CalPaper does not maintain a centralized backend copy of your calendar and weather data, many of these rights are most practically exercised by:

  • Managing or deleting data on your device
  • Disconnecting connected accounts in the App
  • Managing the source data through your calendar provider
  • Withdrawing website analytics consent through the Cookie Settings link on calpaper.eu
  • Contacting the relevant third-party provider whose service you connected

14. Children's Privacy

CalPaper is not specifically directed to children. We do not knowingly operate a service that collects children's personal data through our own backend because the App does not use a CalPaper-operated data collection server. However, use of connected third-party services may still be subject to those providers' own terms and age requirements.

15. Payments and Purchases

CalPaper offers optional premium functionality through a one-time purchase handled by the Microsoft Store.

We do not process or store your payment card information ourselves. Billing, purchase handling, and entitlement verification are performed by Microsoft. The App may locally store purchase state needed to control premium features and may query Microsoft Store services to refresh that status.
